Discover the
life of
Vincent van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh was a Dutch post-impressionist painter who is widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in the history of Western art. Born on March 30, 1853, in the Netherlands, van Gogh's artistic journey was characterized by passion, dedication, and a relentless pursuit of self-expression. Van Gogh's early life was marked by various career paths, including working as an art dealer, teacher, and missionary. However, it was in his late twenties that he decided to devote himself entirely to art. Despite facing numerous challenges and personal struggles, van Gogh remained steadfast in his commitment to creating art.
Van Gogh's unique artistic style emerged through his bold use of color, expressive brushwork, and emotionally charged compositions. His works often depicted landscapes, still lifes, portraits, and self-portraits, capturing the essence of his subjects with raw intensity. Van Gogh's art was characterized by vibrant colors and dynamic textures, conveying a deep sense of emotion and an exploration of the human condition.
Throughout his career, van Gogh produced an extensive body of work, creating over 2,000 artworks, including approximately 900 paintings and 1,100 drawings and sketches. Some of his most renowned pieces include "Starry Night," "Sunflowers," and his self-portraits, which have become iconic representations of his artistic genius.
