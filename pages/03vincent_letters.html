Letters

Not only Vincent painted huindreds of paintings, the also was a avid writter. He needed to share his thoughs and ideas, writting very often to his family, mostly to Theo. During years, Vincent and his younger brother, Theo, communicate by letters. And it is now, the primmary source of knowledge we have from the painter, about his thoughts and art theories. They interchange letters from 1872 and 1890 with a gap from when they lived together in Paris, so there is hundreds of pieces, (around 820), and Theo saved everyone that Vincent sent to him. Vincent only kept a few of them. Is stimated that the wrote around 2000 letters in his life. A third of them in French, after living in Paris he only wrote in Frech. Actually he signed his name by just "Vincent" because french people had a difficult time pronouncing "van Gogh".
There is more than just thoughts in the letters, a lot of them came with drawings and sketches for paintings, he called it "scratches", to gave Theo or an artist friend an idea of what he was working on at the moment. There is around 240 sketches, some of them very quick drawings made with pencil, while others are more detailed, even colored ones or with que colors written in it. The van Gogh Museum has a complete catalog of the letters in here Many of the letters remaiun undated.

Vincent also sent his paiunting by mail to Theo, he gave his brother all his artwork in exchange for a monthly allowance. And then Theo, as an artdealer try tol sell them. Theo also sent other things to his brother, like canvas, brushes and tuve of paint.
There is a book that features around 70 letters from Vincent, its called "Vincent van Gogh: a life in letters"
