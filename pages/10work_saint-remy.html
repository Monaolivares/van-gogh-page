The Man is at Sea, after Demont-Breton

October 1889, oil on canvas. At a private collection. This piece is after another from the french painter Virginie Demont-Breton, "Her Man is Out to Sea" in 1889. Later that year, Vincent made her version of it while at the asylum. During his time in the asylum Van Gogh painted his surroundings but he also spent time re-creating works by other artists. Van Gogh produced some 30 different copies of works by other artists during his time in the asylum in an attempt to be reinvigorated artistically. He did not simply copy the works but he translated them through his own perspective, medium, colors and techniques..
